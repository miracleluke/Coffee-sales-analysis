# Coffee Sales Performance Dashboard

# Project Overview

The Coffee Sales Performance Dashboard is an interactive data visualization project built to analyze coffee shop performance from **March 2024 to March 2025**.
The goal of this project is to transform transactional sales data into actionable business insights that support decision-making in areas such as staffing, marketing, product strategy, and operational planning by providing insights into:
- Total sales performance
- Sales trends over time
- Customer purchasing patterns
- Peak sales hours
- Seasonal performance
- Coffee product performance

The project is divided into two dashboard pages:
1. Overview
2. Records


# Tools and Methodology 

**Tools**
- **Tableau** – Data visualization & interactive dashboard design
- **Excel** – Data cleaning and preparation

**Methodology**
- **Data Cleaning** - This process involved inspecting the dataset for inconsistencies and ensuring correct data format.
- **Data Processsing** - I used Tableau to create calculated fields and parameters so as to uncover deep insights from the dataset.
- **Data Visualization** - I built a 2-paged interactive dashboard in Tableau that shows key metrics and trends to improve decision making in staffing and operational planning.


#  Dashboard

**Overview Page**

The Overview page provides a high level summary of business performance through:
- KPI cards 
- Monthly trend
- Hourly trend
- Season breakdown
- Top performing days
- Parameter filter for sales and quantity 

This page answers questions like:
- When do we sell the most?
- Which season performs best?
- What time should staffing increase?
- Which days are most profitable?
- Which period underperforms?
- Which coffee do sell more?
- What time can staff take breaks? 

**Records Page**

The Records page provides a detailed transactional-level breakdown of daily performance, each record includes:
- Date
- Season
- Day Type
- Day
- Peak Hour
- Top Coffee Product
- Quantity Sold
- Total Sales

This page allows users to:
- Drill into specific days
- Analyze product-level performance
- Compare weekday vs weekend behavior
- Track daily peak-hour trends


# Key Insights 

- The business generates significantly higher revenue on weekdays compared to weekends. This suggests strong weekday customer flow and possible office workers driven sales.
- Sales are fairly evenly distributed throughout the day, with a slight edge toward night and afternoon periods. This indicates consistent traffic across operating hours.
- Autumn generated the highest revenue, while Summer recorded the lowest sales. This indicates seasonal demands, seasonal promotions or product adjustments could be implemented to improve summer performance.
- The monthly trend shows fluctuations throughout the year, with noticeable peaks around early autumn and late winter.
- Early weekdays outperform weekends, suggesting the business benefits from work-week customer traffic.
- The hourly trend shows steady growth from 6 AM and peaks at 10 AM indicating morning rush dominance.
